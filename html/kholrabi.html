Kohlrabi

Brassica oleracea var. gongylodes

Kohlrabi is a member of the cabbage family, and is an easy-to-grow vegetable that is rich in nutrients. It has a bulbous stem that grows above ground, which can be eaten raw or cooked. The leaves are also edible.

Quick Info

  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soil rich in organic matter
  • Water: Regular watering, keeping the soil consistently moist
  • Light: Full sun to partial shade
  • Sowing Method: Direct sow or transplant
  • Growing Days: 55

Planting Instructions

Planting Instructions: Sow seeds 1/4 to 1/2 inch deep, spaced 1 inch apart. Thin to 4-6 inches apart once the seedlings are a few inches tall. For a fall harvest, sow seeds in late summer.

Harvest Instructions

Harvest Instructions: Harvest when the bulbs are 2-3 inches in diameter for the best flavor and texture. Cut the bulb from the stem at ground level.
