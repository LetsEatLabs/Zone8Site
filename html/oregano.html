Oregano

Origanum vulgare

Oregano is a perennial herb with purple flowers and spade-shaped, olive-green leaves. It is a member of the mint family and is closely related to marjoram.

Quick Info

  • Soil: Well-drained soil
  • Water: Moderate
  • Light: Full sun
  • Sowing Method: Direct sow or transplant
  • Growing Days: 90

Planting Instructions

Planting Instructions: Sow seeds or plant transplants in early spring. Space plants 8 to 10 inches apart in an area with full sun and well-drained soil with a pH of 6.5 to 7.0.

Harvest Instructions

Harvest Instructions: Harvest leaves as needed throughout the growing season. For the best flavor, harvest in the morning after the dew has dried.
