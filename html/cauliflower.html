Cauliflower

Brassica oleracea var. botrytis

Cauliflower is a cool-season vegetable that is part of the Brassica family, which includes broccoli, Brussels sprouts, and cabbage. It grows best in cool temperatures with a consistent supply of moisture.

Quick Info

  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soil high in organic matter
  • Water: Regular, consistent watering
  • Light: Full sun
  • Sowing Method: Direct sow or transplant
  • Growing Days: 85

Planting Instructions

Planting Instructions: Sow seeds 1/2 inch deep and 3 inches apart. If transplanting, start seeds indoors 4 to 5 weeks before the last frost date.

Harvest Instructions

Harvest Instructions: Harvest when the heads are compact, white, and firm. Cut the heads off the plant with a large knife.
