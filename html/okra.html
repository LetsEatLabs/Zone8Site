Okra

Abelmoschus esculentus

Okra is a warm-season vegetable that grows well in southern and central regions. It produces long, slender pods that are harvested and used in soups, stews, and side dishes.

Quick Info

  • Soil: Well-drained, fertile soil with a pH of 6.5 to 7.5
  • Water: Regular watering, especially during dry spells
  • Light: Full sun
  • Sowing Method: Direct sow
  • Growing Days: 50

Planting Instructions

Planting Instructions: Sow seeds 1 inch deep and 12 inches apart in rows that are 3 feet apart. Soak seeds overnight to help speed up germination.

Harvest Instructions

Harvest Instructions: Harvest pods when they are 2-3 inches long for the best flavor and texture. Harvest regularly to encourage more production.
