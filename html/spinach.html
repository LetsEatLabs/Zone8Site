Spinach

Spinacia oleracea

Spinach is a leafy green flowering plant native to central and western Asia. It is of the order Caryophyllales, family Amaranthaceae, subfamily Chenopodioideae. Its leaves are a common edible vegetable consumed either fresh, or after storage using preservation techniques by canning, freezing, or dehydration. It may be eaten cooked or raw, and the taste can be somewhat bitter.

Quick Info

  • Soil: Well-drained soil rich in organic matter with a pH of 6.5 to 7.0.
  • Water: Regular watering, keeping the soil moist but not soggy
  • Light: Full sun to partial shade
  • Sowing Method: Direct sow
  • Growing Days: 45

Planting Instructions

Planting Instructions: Sow seeds 1/2 inch deep and 2-3 inches apart in rows 12-18 inches apart. Thin seedlings to 4-6 inches apart once they have two true leaves.

Harvest Instructions

Harvest Instructions: Harvest leaves from the outside in when they are large enough to eat. Spinach can be harvested as a 'cut and come again' crop.
